Juneteenth Reflection

Conversation and Reflection Prompts

A short set of questions to help spark thoughtful conversations about freedom, history, gratitude, and hope.

1

What does freedom mean to you?

Share a simple example of a freedom you are thankful for in your daily life.

2

What can we learn from listening to other people's stories?

Talk about how listening carefully can help us grow in empathy, understanding, and respect for different experiences.

3

Who inspires you?

Name a person from your family, school, neighborhood, or history who shows courage, kindness, or leadership.

4

What traditions help people feel connected?

Think about food, music, stories, or celebrations that bring your family or community together.

5

What kind of future do you hope to help build?

Share one way you can help make your school, workplace, family, or community more welcoming and fair.

6

What is one thing you want to keep learning?

End by naming one question, story, or part of Juneteenth history you would like to explore more deeply.

These prompts are meant to open conversation, not rush it. Even one thoughtful question can lead to a meaningful moment together.

Honoring the past, dancing in the present, and building the future. Happy Juneteenth!
