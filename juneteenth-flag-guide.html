Juneteenth Flag Guide

A quick reference for the Juneteenth flag and the meaning behind its main symbols.

Red, white, and blue

The colors mirror the American flag and emphasize that enslaved people and their descendants are Americans.

The star

The center star points to Texas, where Juneteenth began, and also represents the spread of freedom beyond one place to the entire nation.

The burst around the star

The outline around the star symbolizes a nova, or a new star. This represents a fresh beginning and new possibilities for African Americans.

The curved horizon

The sweeping curved line across the flag represents a "new horizon" - the promising future, opportunities, and unfolding journey ahead for Black Americans.

Common extra note

Many versions of the flag also include the date June 19, 1865, marking the day news of freedom reached enslaved people in Galveston, Texas.
