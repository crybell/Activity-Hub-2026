Did you know?

Juneteenth (short for “June Nineteenth”) marks the day when federal troops arrived in Galveston, Texas in 1865 to formally inform enslaved people that the Emancipation Proclamation had legally freed them two and a half years earlier. Juneteenth honors independence for African Americans. On June 17, 2021, it officially became a federal holiday.
